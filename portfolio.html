Writing

A Story Of Love and Adventure

This piece was written for my Spring 2025 Mythology class and reimagines a mythic love story set against the backdrop of a divine conflict. Drawing on themes of forbidden love, divine intervention, and heroic perseverance, I crafted an original myth that feels rooted in classical Greek storytelling. The narrative follows Elias and Katerina, two lovers separated by a sacred dispute and a river created by Zeus himself. Inspired by mythological archetypes and the symbolic guidance of gods like Hermes and Hera, the story blends emotional depth with traditional mythic structure. This assignment allowed me to explore world-building, symbolism, and the timeless patterns that shape ancient and modern narratives.

Montage Theory

This essay was written for my Non-Linear Video Editing class and examines why the training montage in Rocky IV remains such an iconic example of montage editing. Using Eisenstein’s theory and Karen Pearlman’s concepts from Cutting Rhythms, I analyzed how the scene creates meaning through contrast—Rocky’s natural, grit-filled training versus Drago’s cold, machine-driven routine. I focused on rhythm, pacing, and emotional beats, and how intuitive editing choices tell a story without relying on dialogue. This project strengthened my understanding of montage as a storytelling tool and deepened my appreciation for how editing shapes audience emotion.

Why Webdesign Fundamentals Still Matter In The Age Of Templates And AI

This piece was written for my Writing on the Web course, where I explored why design fundamentals still matter even with the rise of templates, page builders, and AI tools. Drawing on ideas from Steve Krug’s Don’t Make Me Think and articles about the overwhelm of modern web development, I explained how hierarchy, clarity, consistency, and accessibility are still at the core of usable design. The assignment also connected to my own experience learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript—showing how tools can speed things up, but they don’t replace real understanding. Fundamentals are what keep layouts intentional, fix issues when templates break, and create a clearer experience for users.
